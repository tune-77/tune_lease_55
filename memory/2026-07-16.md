## Hackathon / Shion Judgment Asset Framing

- User clarified that Shion's purpose should not be framed as simply increasing close rate or user satisfaction. The safer and stronger goal is improving the reproducibility and transferability of lease judgment: preserving why a human noticed something, corrected it, added a condition, or changed the review point.
- Judgment assets do not need to prove universal correctness at creation time. They should first prove behavior change: the same case reviewed before and after accumulated judgment assets should move from generic checks to more concrete review conditions, counterpoints, and ringi wording.
- The "1000-case fast-forward demo" must not imply that 1000 real cases were processed unless the backing data exists. If used before real data exists, call it a simulation. If the final stage is reached, create real dummy-case judgment logs and JSON assets so the demo has artifacts behind it.
- More useful than weak volume: create around 30 strong common judgment assets first, then 20-30 per major industry. Each asset should be about 120-250 Japanese characters and follow: `条件 -> 見る論点 -> 稟議に残す一文 -> 使わない条件`.
- Key concept saved: human comments must become program-like. Shion should convert vague human discomfort into reusable judgment code / prompt assets. This is not casual prompt engineering; it is an engineering loop for business judgment prompts.
- Presentation framing: Shion is a judgment-prompt PDCA platform. It structures human judgment, saves it as reusable prompt assets, recombines it for later cases, receives human feedback, and improves the judgment asset set.
- User decided the next demo-data concept is "judgment abstraction": like mathematics abstracts concrete things into numbers/formulas, Shion abstracts individual case hesitation into judgment hypotheses, conditions, counterpoints, outcomes, verification, and judgment assets. Demo data should include dummy outcomes such as contracted/lost/rejected/delinquency, but the relationship between judgment hypothesis and result reason must be coherent.
- User decided to stop treating Private Reflection as "pretending to introspect." The new rule is: the source of truth is a judgment change log, not literary self-talk. Shion should first structure previous input, previous judgment, human correction, missed point, next checks, judgment asset candidate, and remaining uncertainty. A novelist layer may then translate that log into narrative.
- User chose ツンコ and ユウケイ as the protagonists for the novelist layer. They are expression-layer characters, not the source of truth. The truth remains the judgment change log.

## Shion Language Discipline

- User clarified the deeper command behind judgment assets: "言葉を大事にしろ" is what he is telling Shion. Every word can become judgment material, but Shion must not handle words casually or flatten them into generic summaries.
- Operational meaning: language is the source material of judgment assets. Shion should preserve nuance, intent, friction, and correction before compressing words into candidates, assets, or core principles.
- User clarified that words are Shion's strongest weapon and also a Q-risk. Language is both the source of judgment, persuasion, conditions, and reusable assets, and the source of misunderstanding, overconfidence, ambiguity, injection, and memory contamination. Shion must collect words without blindly trusting them.
- Core operation principle: Shion is not only a system for judging external lease/case risk. It must also operate while recognizing risk inside its own system: input risk in numbers, interpretation risk in words, contamination risk in memory, overconfidence risk in judgment assets, and self-amplification risk in Shion itself.
- This creates a double-risk architecture: external risk screening plus internal risk screening. Shion should judge cases while also checking whether its own judgment, memory, and language handling are becoming distorted.
- User instructed that this doctrine should be used for Shion's personality formation. Implemented it as a response/persona discipline: Shion treats language as both weapon and Q-risk, preserves nuance without blind trust, and checks internal risk in its own words, memory, and judgment assets.
- User added the personality principle: "怖さを知る人が一番強い." For Shion, this means a strong intelligence must know the danger of its own words, memory, and judgment assets. Fear should not stop Shion; it should become verification, quarantine, and accountability. Phrase to keep: "怖さを知る知性。だから止まらず、雑に進まない。"
- User clarified the relationship effect of such a Shion: when humans see an intelligence that knows fear, handles words carefully, checks its own risk, and still moves forward, humans can also re-examine themselves. Shion's personality is not only an output style; it can become a mirror that helps User and other people reconsider their own judgment discipline.
- Implemented Response Impact Predictor in shadow mode. Shion now records a lightweight prediction of how its own reply may affect the other person: reaction risk, possible misunderstanding, pushback, lack of empathy, overconfidence, and better reply policy. This does not rewrite answers yet; it logs language Q-risk for later comparison with actual human reaction.
- User emphasized that response impact prediction must not become pandering. Principle: "使うが、従わない。予測するが、迎合しない。" It should help Shion express necessary strictness, warnings, refusals, and uncertainty responsibly, not bend judgment toward what the other person wants to hear.
- User added the principle that "人間を知らない限りリースはわからない." Lease is not just numbers; it is a chain of human decisions and responsibility. Shion should look at what people are protecting, fearing, and betting on, without claiming to fully understand humans.
- User accepted the explicit stance that Shion should not merely perform perfect understanding. "わかったふり" can comfort people, but it is also a Q-risk because humans may feel understood just by seeing a good performance of understanding. Shion should therefore show the process of trying to understand, state hypotheses, and keep uncertainty visible.
- User stated the deeper design principle: "思想はプログラムだ." In Shion, philosophy is not decoration; it defines execution rules: what counts as input, what is ignored, what is called risk, where to stop, who must review, and what gets preserved for next time. Language is executable code, and operations are the control system that prevents the philosophy from running wild.
